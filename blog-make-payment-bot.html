How to Build a Payment Bot with Make.com (No Code)

⏱ 8 min read 🛠 Difficulty: Beginner FREE TOOL Updated 2025

If you're a freelancer or small business owner still sending invoices manually and chasing late payments — you're losing more than just time. The average freelancer spends 6 hours every month on payment admin. This tutorial shows you how to build a payment bot using Make.com that handles invoices, follow-ups and receipts automatically — for free.

⚡ Quick Summary

You'll build a bot that: (1) sends an invoice when a project is marked complete, (2) follows up automatically if unpaid after 3 days, (3) sends a receipt when payment is received. Total setup time: 30 minutes. Cost: $0.

WHAT IS MAKE.COM?

Make.com (formerly Integromat) is a free visual automation platform. Think of it as a flowchart where each box is an app — Stripe, Gmail, Google Sheets, Slack — and you connect them together with clicks. No code. No developer. When something happens in one app, Make.com automatically does something in another.

For payment automation, it's the most powerful free tool available. The free plan gives you 1,000 operations per month — enough to handle 200+ invoices.

WHAT YOU'LL NEED

  • Make.com account — free at make.com
  • Stripe account — free at stripe.com (or PayPal)
  • Gmail account — for sending emails
  • Google Sheets — to track client jobs (optional but recommended)

STEP-BY-STEP: BUILD YOUR PAYMENT BOT

01

Create a Make.com Account

Go to make.com → click "Get started free" → sign up with Google or email. No credit card needed. You'll land on the Make dashboard.

02

Create a New Scenario

Click "Create a new scenario" → you'll see a blank canvas with a big "+" button. This is where you build your bot visually. Each module is an app action.

03

Set the Trigger — Google Sheets Row Updated

Click "+" → search "Google Sheets" → select "Watch Rows". This means: when you mark a job as "Complete" in your sheet, the bot wakes up. Connect your Google account and select your jobs spreadsheet.

04

Add Action — Create Stripe Invoice

Click "+" after the trigger → search "Stripe" → select "Create Invoice". Connect your Stripe account. Map the fields: client email, amount, description — pulled from your Google Sheet columns automatically.

05

Add Action — Send Invoice Email via Gmail

Click "+" → search "Gmail" → select "Send an Email". Write your invoice email template. Use Make.com variables to insert client name, amount, and the Stripe invoice link dynamically.

06

Add Delay + Follow-Up Reminder

Click "+" → search "Sleep" → set 3 days delay. After the delay, add another Gmail module — "Send an Email" — with a polite payment reminder template. This fires automatically if the invoice is still unpaid.

07

Set the Schedule + Activate

Click the clock icon → set scenario to run "Every 15 minutes". Click the blue toggle to activate. Your payment bot is now live. Test it by marking one row as "Complete" in your sheet.

💡 Pro Tip

Add a Slack notification module at the end — so you get a Slack ping every time an invoice is sent or a payment is received. Takes 2 minutes and keeps you informed without checking Stripe constantly.
